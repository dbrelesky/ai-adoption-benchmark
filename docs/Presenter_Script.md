# Presenter Script: Benchmarking AI Adoption Across Teams

**Total time: 30 minutes**
**Format: 12 min presenting, 18 min discussion**

---

- [ ] Drop the poll link in the meeting chat or Slack channel: **[POLL URL]**
- [ ] Have the leave-behind doc ready to share after the session
- [ ] Open presentation in slideshow mode
- [ ] Have the poll results page open on your phone so you can reference your own scores

## SLIDE 1 — Title (30 sec)

**On screen:** BENCHMARKING / AI Adoption Across Teams

**Say:** "Thanks for having me. I'm going to take about 12 minutes to walk through some data, and then I want to spend the back half of this in conversation. The question on the table: in this wave of AI tooling, are we keeping pace — or are we getting lapped?"

**Then advance.**

---

## SLIDE 2 — Context + Poll (3 min)

> **INTERACTIVE MOMENT #1: Shout-it-out poll**

**On screen:** Left side = your context card. Right side = poll question.

**Say (left side, 60 sec):** "Quick context on me — Darren, EVP Product at CBS Digital. About 250 people across Product, Design, Engineering. We've got pockets of AI adoption — some teams shipping AI features, others still experimenting. I'm feeling the tension that I think a lot of you feel too: how do I keep 250 people motivated in this Cambrian explosion while being realistic about what we can actually ship?"

**Say (right side, transition to poll):** "Before I show you any data — quick gut check."

**Read the question aloud:** "What percentage of your engineering output do you think is AI-authored today?"

**Facilitate:** "Just shout it out. No wrong answers."

**PAUSE.** Let 4-5 people answer. Note the range out loud: "OK, I'm hearing everything from 5% to 35%. Keep your number in mind — I'm about to show you what 135,000 developers actually measured."

**Advance.**

---

## SLIDE 3 — The Numbers (3 min)

**On screen:** 22% big stat on left. Three industry benchmarks on right.

**Say:** "Here's the number: 22% of merged code is now AI-authored. That's across 135,000 developers surveyed by DX in Q4 2025."

**Point to right side:** "Some context around that. 88% of organizations are using AI in at least one function. 82% of developers use AI tools weekly. And Gartner says 40% of enterprise apps will have AI agents by end of this year."

**Point to bottom bar:** "The leading companies are already at 41-42%. The average developer is saving 3.6 hours a week. But there's a caution zone — above 40% AI code, you start seeing 20-30% rework rates and rising technical debt. The sweet spot is 25-40%."

**Bridge:** "But code is only part of the picture. Let me show you what it looks like when you expand the lens."

**Advance.**

---

## SLIDE 4 — Score Your Organization (4 min)

> **INTERACTIVE MOMENT #2: Live poll on phones**

**On screen:** Four dimension cards with industry benchmarks + poll prompt.

**Say:** "I dropped a link in the chat. Pull it up on your phone."

**PAUSE 15 seconds** to let people open the link.

**Say:** "You're going to score your organization 1 to 5 on five dimensions. While you fill it out, let me walk you through what the industry benchmarks look like."

**Walk the four cards (30 sec each):**

1. **Engineering:** "Industry average is 22% AI-authored code. Leaders are at 41-42%. If your team has Copilot access but it's mostly organic, you're probably a 2. If AI is integrated into your CI/CD, you're a 3 or 4."

2. **Testing & QA:** "89% of orgs are piloting AI in QA, but industry automated test coverage has plateaued at just 25%. If you're using AI to generate some tests ad-hoc, that's a 2. If it's in your pipeline, 3 or 4."

3. **Consumer Insights:** "68% of sales and marketing teams use AI daily. 49% of consumers actively want AI-personalized recommendations. Are you using AI for ad-hoc analysis, or is it powering real-time personalization in prod?"

4. **Talent & Readiness:** "Here's the sobering one. Only 20% of organizations say their talent is highly prepared for AI. The skills gap is the number one barrier. Are your people trained, or just given tool access?"

**Say:** "Take another 30 seconds to finish up. Hit 'See Your Score' when you're done."

**PAUSE** for people to finish.

**Bridge:** "Whatever number you got — that tells you where you sit on the maturity model I'm about to show you."

**Advance.**

---

## SLIDE 5 — Where Should We Be (2 min)

**On screen:** Three-phase maturity model: Experimenting → Embedding → Scaling

**Say:** "Based on the data, I see three phases. Phase 1, Experimenting — individual AI tool use, sub-15% code, no strategy. This is where the majority of companies still are."

**Gesture to Phase 2:** "Phase 2, Embedding — this is where I believe most of us in this room need to be right now. AI in standard workflows, 20-30% AI code, AI in your CI/CD, literacy programs actually running."

**Gesture to Phase 3:** "Phase 3, Scaling — this is our target for end of 2026. Agents in production, 30-40% code range, AI-first test strategy, dedicated roles with real budget."

**Connect to poll:** "If your score was 5-10, you're in Phase 1. 11-18, you're in Phase 2. 19-25, you're in Phase 3. How did that match your gut?"

**Advance.**

---

## SLIDE 6 — Framework Proposal (1.5 min)

**On screen:** Five-dimension scoring framework with weights.

**Say:** "Here's my proposal. You just took the first version of this assessment on your phones. I want to formalize it as a council-wide benchmark."

**Walk the five dimensions quickly** — don't re-explain, just name and weight: "Code & Delivery at 25%. Quality & Testing, 20%. Consumer Intelligence, 20%. Talent & Culture, 20% — because the data shows this is where we're all weakest. And Governance at 15%."

**The ask:** "The idea is simple: do this quarterly, share scores anonymously across the council, and use the comparison to learn from each other. It's the scorecard you just filled out, done every quarter."

**Advance.**

---

## SLIDE 7 — Discussion (15-18 min)

> **INTERACTIVE MOMENT #3: Open discussion**

**On screen:** Four numbered questions.

**Say:** "That's the data. Let's talk. I have four questions but we don't have to get through all of them."

**Start with Q1:** "Where did your scores surprise you? Anyone willing to share?"

**Let this run 3-4 minutes.** Draw out specific examples. If someone scores low on talent, ask what they're doing about it.

**Move to Q2:** "Is a council-wide benchmark something we'd actually commit to? I don't want it to be a nice idea that dies in this room."

**Then Q3 if time:** "What dimensions am I missing from the framework?"

**Close (final 2 min):** "I'm going to send around the full scorecard and the leave-behind doc after this. I need three volunteers who'd be willing to fill it out formally for next quarter so we have real data to compare. Who's in?"

**Get names.** Thank the room.
